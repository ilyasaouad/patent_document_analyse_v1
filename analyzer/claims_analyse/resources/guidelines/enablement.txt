**SOURCE:**

* Patentstyret Guidelines, Part C, Chapter II (Tilstrekkelig beskrivelse)
* Norwegian Patents Act § 8 (2)

---

### 1. General requirement for sufficient disclosure

"The invention must be described so clearly and completely that a person skilled in the art can carry it out."

---

### 2. Level of detail required

"The description must provide sufficient information for the skilled person to carry out the invention without undue burden. A certain amount of trial and error is acceptable, provided this does not require inventive effort."

---

### 3. Result-to-be-achieved objection

"A common deficiency arises where the application merely states a result to be achieved without specifying how this can be achieved across the entire claimed scope. A mere assertion or conceptual description without technical measures or parameters is insufficient."

---

### 4. Scope of enablement

"The invention must be capable of being carried out within the entire scope covered by the claims."

---

### 5. Reproducibility

"The invention must be reproducible in a reliable manner; otherwise it is not considered sufficiently disclosed."

---

### 6. Skilled person and common general knowledge

"The skilled person may rely on common general knowledge to supplement the description."
