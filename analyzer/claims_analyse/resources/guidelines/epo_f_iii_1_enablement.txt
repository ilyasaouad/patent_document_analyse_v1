[EPO GUIDELINES FOR EXAMINATION - PART F-III, 1]
[Topic: Sufficiency of disclosure / Enablement | Art. 83 EPC]
[Version: October 2025 | Source: https://www.epo.org/en/legal/guidelines-epc/2025/f_iii_1.html]

§1.1 General Principle
"The European patent application must disclose the invention in a manner sufficiently clear and complete for it to be carried out by a person skilled in the art."

§1.2 Level of details
"The disclosure must provide sufficient information for the skilled person to reproduce the invention without undue burden. Some trial and error is acceptable, provided the skilled person is not required to perform inventive step."

§1.3 Objections under Art. 83
"A common objection is that the application only describes a result to be achieved without providing the means to hit that result in the entire claimed range. A mere assertion or conceptual description without technical parameters or steps is insufficient."
